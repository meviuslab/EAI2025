Invited Keynote Speakers



Fabio Nonato de Paula, Amazon Web Services
Short bio: Dr. Fabio Nonato de Paula is a Senior Manager of Applied Science at Amazon Web Services, where he leads the Perimeter Protection Science team, focused on securing large‑scale generative‑AI and cloud‑networking services. His group develops defenses against adversarial abuse of language models, builds autonomous threat‑detection pipelines, and advances ethics‑aware, proactive security mechanisms for AI systems in production. Before AWS, Dr. Nonato directed Engineering and Data Science organizations at Intel, GE, and Shell, delivering patented solutions in anomaly detection, autonomous energy‑trading, and knowledge‑discovery platforms adopted across Fortune 100 enterprises. His research interests include Bayesian modeling, probabilistic deep learning, graph‑based threat analysis, and high‑performance computing for machine learning. He holds a Ph.D. and M.Sc. in Computational Methods for Mechanical Engineering from the University of Campinas, Brazil, and brings more than 16 years of experience building trustworthy AI for safety‑critical domains.
Jundong Li, University of Virginia
Short bio: Dr. Li is an Assistant Professor at the University of Virginia. His research interests are data mining and machine learning, focusing on graph machine learning, trustworthy/safe machine learning, and, more recently, large language models. He has published over 150 papers in high-impact venues (including KDD, NeruIPS, ICLR, WWW, SIGIR, WSDM, IJCAI, AAAI, SIGIR, ACL, EMNLP, CIKM, ICDM, SDM, ECML-PKDD, CSUR, TPAMI, TKDE, TKDD, TIST, etc.), with over 10,000 citation count. He has won several prestigious awards, including SIGKDD Best Research Paper Award (2022), NSF CAREER Award (2022), PAKDD Early Career Research Award (2023), JP Morgan Chase Faculty Research Award (2021 \& 2022), Cisco Faculty Research Award (2021), and being selected for the AAAI New Faculty Highlights roster (2021). His group's research is generously supported by NSF (CAREER, III, SaTC, SAI), DOE, Commonwealth Cyber Initiative, Jefferson Lab, JP Morgan, Cisco, and Snap.
Gabbrielle Johnson, Claremont McKenna College
Short bio: Dr. Johnson is an Assistant Professor of Philosophy at Claremont McKenna College. Their work focuses on the Philosophy of Psychology (particularly perception and social cognition), Philosophy of Mind, Philosophy of Science, and Philosophy of Technology. Her current research explores theories of bias beyond individual agents, examining how biases manifest in domains such as machine learning systems, scientific inference, statistical models of the visual perceptual system, and broader societal and structural injustices.
